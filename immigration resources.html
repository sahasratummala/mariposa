Immigration Resources

Poverty rate was 14.1 percent among immigrants compared to 9.8 percent for the U.S.-born residents.

To those interested in the discrimination, legal issues, and recurring problems within the Immigrant community we share resources that provide immigrants with support,information to become more involved, and those who are curious about helping Immigrants:

Raices

Raices defends the rights of immigrants and refugees. They are a non-profit agency that provides education, counseling, and legal assistance to immigrant communities. Raices is constantly bringing attention to actions that affect Immigrants and informative information to help those affected. It is a great organization to follow to stay informed!

Immi

Immi is a non-profit organization that provides online screening for immigrants qualification to stay in the U.S. , legal help, and a learning center. The Immi website is easy to use and has an option for both English and Spanish speakers. Their legal help is flexible since it works with several organizations around the country that provide help, they can also accommodate those in detention centers. In the learning center you can find information about options available for immigrants who want to stay in the U.S, as well as general information to aid you during the process.

ilrc

Ilrc is a non-profit organization that offers resources that are easy to find where immigrants can look to find information that can benefit their affected area. They also offer different resource types that can fit people with different needs. If you are looking to stay informed, ilrc also has resources that do just that. This website is a perfect tool for those just getting started in their immigration journey!
