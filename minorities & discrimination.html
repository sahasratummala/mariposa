Minorities & Discrimination

About four-in-ten Black and Asian adults say people have acted as if they were uncomfortable around them because of their race

Minorities are often subjected to discrimination in work, school, and everyday life. Although officials release statements reminding that acting against anybody because of their race, ethnicity and national origin is racism and can occasionally be referred to as a hate crime the reports don't stop. After the start of coronavirus an advocacy group collected 1,100 reports of asian discrimination.

The crown act is now pushing to be passed in all 50 states of the U.S in hopes of making it illegal to discriminate against hair. The crown act started after a white staffer of Dina Neal, the first African American woman elected to the Nevada Assembly, was told her hair was “inappropriate” and “unprofessional”. Discrimination against black hairstyles does not just happen in work settings, children have had their hair cut or removed from classrooms because of their hair. 1 in 2 black children have faced this type of discrimination before five years old. Crown act hopes to end hair discrimination and make an equitable space for black individuals.
